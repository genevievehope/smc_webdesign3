The 5 Classes

Amphibians
cold-blooded vertebrate animals which begin life in water and mature on land
Birds
endothermic vertebrates which have feathers, toothless beaked jaws, a four-chambered heart, a strong yet lightweight skeleton, and lay eggs
Fish
gill-bearing aquatic craniate (skull-bearing) animals that lack limbs
Mammals
a warm-blooded vertebrate animal which possesses hair or fur, secretes milk, and (typically) births live young
Reptiles
cold-blooded, vertbrate animals which have scales and typically lay hard-shelled eggs to reproduce
